AI POC: CKO Due Today Test

Three A/B test variations for the Checkout payment plan screen. Each variant emphasizes the amount due today (first installment) instead of the full order amount — to reduce drop-off after risk approval. Both single-plan and multi-plan states are shown for mobile and desktop.

Product owner: John Kresse
Screen: /payment (CKO)
Components: PaymentPlanSpotlight · PaymentPlanSelector
Experiment: Optimizely (to be wired up post-variation selection)
Mock data: $250.00 order · 4 payments of $62.50 (Pay-in-4) · 2 payments of $125.00 (Pay-in-2)

Variation A — Subtle "Due Today" Label Swap Lowest effort

Minimal change to the current layout. "Due today" replaces "4 bi-weekly payments of" as the primary label above the large dollar amount. A smaller secondary line ("4 bi-weekly payments of $62.50") preserves plan structure for transparency. The visual weight of the amount stays the same as control. Lowest dev lift — pure copy and label change. Matches how Klarna frames its checkout.

B
Variation B — Bold "Due Today" Hero High emphasis

Maximum visual impact. The first installment amount becomes the dominant hero element — rendered in Zip's brand purple at 44px with a purple pill badge labeled "DUE TODAY" above it. The installment count and total order amount drop to muted secondary text. This most aggressively reinforces the Zip value prop ("pay less today") and most closely mirrors how Afterpay emphasizes their first payment.

C
Variation C — Split "Due Today / Order Total" Panel Explicit contrast

A two-column card layout that places "Due today" (in brand purple) and "Order total" (in muted grey) side by side. The explicit visual contrast between what the customer pays now vs. the full amount directly communicates Zip's core value prop. In the multi-plan radio list, the "due today" amount is right-aligned in purple — making plan comparison by first payment effortless. Full order amount remains visible, supporting compliance and transparency.
